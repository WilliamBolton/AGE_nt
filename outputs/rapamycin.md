## 5) Limitations of this Run

*   **Limited Human Safety Data:** The evidence base lacks robust, high-quality human safety data (Level 1) for rapamycin, leading to a gating penalty.
*   **Lack of Direct Human Endpoint Data:** While human RCTs (Level 2) exist, they primarily focus on healthspan metrics or specific conditions rather than direct, validated human ageing endpoints (Level 3).
*   **Inclusion of Lower-Level Evidence:** The score includes Level 6 evidence (e.g., patents, news) primarily for supporting purposes, which adds some uncertainty.
*   **Potential Publication Bias:** Meta-analyses (Level 1) can be susceptible to publication bias, potentially overestimating the true effect size.
*   **Focus on Rapamycin:** The evidence exclusively focuses on rapamycin, not its derivatives or other mTOR inhibitors, limiting the scope of the conclusion.
